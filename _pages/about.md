<div class="ds-hero">
  <h2>Hi, I am Diana! 👋</h2>
  <p>I am an applied mathematician at UERJ, and I work with combinatorics, graph theory, algorithms, and computational complexity. My favorite problems live where elegant mathematics meets hard computational questions.</p>
</div>

I am an Associate Professor in the Department of Applied Mathematics at the Institute of Mathematics and Statistics of the Universidade do Estado do Rio de Janeiro. I am also a permanent faculty member and Deputy Coordinator of the Graduate Program in Computational Sciences and Mathematical Modeling, and I serve as Editor-in-Chief of *Cadernos do IME - Serie Matematica*.

My research is mostly about graphs: colorings, total colorings, equitable colorings, snarks, matchings, fullerene graphs, graph games, algorithms, and complexity. I like problems that are simple to state, beautiful to draw, and surprisingly difficult to solve.

I am a CNPq Research Productivity Fellow, Level 2, a UERJ Prociência Fellow, a FAPERJ Young Scientist of Our State, and an affiliated member of the Brazilian Academy of Sciences. I received the 2017 L'Oreal/UNESCO/ABC For Women in Science Award in Mathematical Sciences.

I also care deeply about education and outreach. I work with Math en Jeans, Meninas Olimpicas do IMPA, and OME - OBMEP Mirim na Escola, connecting mathematics with students, teachers, and new generations of researchers.

This site gathers my research, publications, teaching, professional activities, awards, scientific events, editorial work, and supervision.
